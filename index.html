All About Trees!

Click the buttons to learn fun facts! 🌲

A tree is a very big plant! 🌱

Trees have a hard trunk made of wood. Branches grow from the trunk. High up on the branches, leaves grow and catch the sunlight!

A tree's roots stretch underground to drink water and hold the tree safe in the ground. 💧

Trees can live for many, many years! Some trees have lived for thousands of years! That's much longer than people! 🎂

Trees give us fresh air to breathe. They also give homes to birds and animals! 🐦

👑

Crown

The top of the tree! It has all the leaves and branches. Like a leafy hat! 🎩

🪵

Trunk

The tall, strong middle part. It holds up the whole tree! Like a strong body. 💪

🌱

Roots

Roots connect the tree to the ground. They drink up water and hold the tree in place. 💧

Roots connect the tree to the ground. 🪴

They spread far underground, reaching out in many directions to find water. Roots are like the tree's drinking straws! 💧

Roots also store the tree's food. They save energy for when the tree needs it most, like in winter or during dry times.

Roots can be big or small. Some roots are thick and strong. Others are very, very thin—so tiny that some roots can only be seen with a microscope! 🔬

The trunk supports the tree. It's like the tree's backbone! 💪 It holds up all the branches and leaves.

Some trunks reach the top of the tree. Others split into many branches as they grow up. Each tree is different!

Most trunks have five layers. 🎂 The outer layers are called bark. The bark covers the trunk and protects the inner layers from cold, sun, and bugs.

One layer forms rings. 🔄 The more rings a tree has, the older it is! You can count the rings to find out the tree's age. Isn't that cool? 🔍

Branches and leaves make up the crown. 👑 They give the tree its shape. That's why trees look so different from each other!

Leaves come in many shapes and sizes. 🍃 Some are large, flat, and thin like paper. You can see light shine through them!

Others are hard and narrow, like needles. 🌲 These are on evergreen trees. They help the tree stay warm in winter.

🍁

Deciduous Trees

These trees lose their leaves in autumn. The leaves turn red, orange, and yellow first! 🎨

Why? In winter, there is less sun. The tree goes to sleep to save energy. New leaves grow back in spring! 🌸

Examples:

🌳 Cassia

🌳 Japanese Maple

🌳 Teak

🌲

Evergreen Trees

These trees stay green all year! They keep their leaves or needles even in winter. ❄️

Why? Their leaves are tough and waxy. This helps them stay safe from cold and snow! 💚

Many evergreens live in tropical places like Thailand. 🌴 It's warm there, so they don't lose their leaves!

Examples:

🌳 Mango

🌳 Coconut

🌳 Ficus

🌳 Tree!

Trees start as tiny seeds. With water, sun, and soil, they grow into big, tall trees!

Trees make their own food using sunlight! This is called photosynthesis (foh-toh-SIN-thuh-sis). 🌞

Each year, the trunk grows a new ring inside. You can count the rings to find out how old a tree is! 🔍

🏆

The tallest trees in the world are taller than 30-storey buildings! They are called Redwoods.

🎂

Some trees are over 5,000 years old! They were alive when the pyramids were built.

🍎

Trees give us yummy foods like apples, oranges, cherries, and nuts!

🏠

One big tree can be home to hundreds of animals: birds, squirrels, bugs, and more!

💨

Trees clean our air! They breathe in bad air and breathe out fresh air for us.

Trees are super important for our planet! Here's why:

🌡️

🦉
